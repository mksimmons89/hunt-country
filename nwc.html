Nuisance Wildlife Control


Hunt Country takes an integrated approach to managing your properties nuisance wildlife problem. We have extensive field experience with the lethal and non lethal control techniques for wildlife that may wreak havoc on your property. Some nuisance wildlife species include:

  • Beaver (Castor Canadensis)
  • Muskrats (Ondarata zibethica)
  • Wood Chuck (Marmota monax)
  • Rock Dove – Pigeons (Columba livia)
  • Deer (Odocoileus virginianus)
  • Geese (Branta canadensis)


Resident Goose Management


Increasing resident Canada Goose populations can negatively effect properties by increasing nutrient loading of water bodies, increasing erosion due to shore line grazing and bank destabilization, and by stunting native aquatic vegetation growth. Hunt country has extensive hands on experience with managing resident Canada Geese. We are experienced with coordinating with the US Fish and Wildlife Service and the Virginia Department of Game and Inland Fisheries to obtain the necessary permits to carry out both lethal and non-lethal goose control.


What Options Are Out There?


There is no one single “silver bullet” to control resident goose populations. Hunt Country offers a variety of methods to help limit the damage geese cause to your property. Our methods include:


  1. Harassment- Designed to discourage geese from using property
  2. Habitat Modifications – steps taken to make property less attractive to geese
  3. Nest and Egg Treatment – Maintains sustainable adult population without allowing the recruitment of goslings
  4. Overhead Gridding
  5. Round ups – capture and euthanasia of birds during flightless period
  6. Controlled Hunts –Generates revenue for property while reducing goose population
